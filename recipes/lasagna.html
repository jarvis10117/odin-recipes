lasagna

Lasagna, also known by the plural form lasagne, is a type of pasta made in very wide, flat sheets. In Italian cuisine it is used to make the dish lasagna by stacking layers of pasta, alternating with fillings such as ragù, béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses, and seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • 8-10 lasagna sheets (no-boil if possible)
  • 2 cups tomato sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese (grated)
  • 1 cup ricotta or paneer (crumbled, optional)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
  • Salt & pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Boil lasagna sheets if not pre-cooked (about 8-10 mins).
  3. Grease baking dish with olive oil or butter.
  4. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on the bottom.
  5. Place 2-3 lasagna sheets over the sauce.
  6. Spread tomato sauce on top of the sheets.
  7. Add ricotta or paneer (if using).
  8. Sprinkle mozzarella cheese.
  9. Repeat layers (sheets → sauce → cheese) until dish is full.
  10. Finish with a top layer of sauce and extra cheese.
  11. Cover with foil and bake for 25 mins.
  12. Remove foil and bake another 10-15 mins until golden.
  13. Let rest for 5 mins before cutting.
